Time-Dependent Perturbation Theory#

Motivation#

After we have learned what wavepackets are, we now want to answer the questions:

  • How can quantum wavepackets be created?

Broadly speaking, you can create a quantum wavepacket in the same way you create classical waves â€šÃ„Ã® by perturbing the system!

Thus, we will examine the influence of different kinds of perturbations on the time-evolution of quantum systems.

We consider the time evolution of a quantum system under the influence of a time-dependent perturbation.

\[ |\psi(t) angle = U_0(t, t_0) |\psi(t_0) angle \]

\[ |\psi(t) angle = U_0(t, t_0) |\psi_I(t) angle \ |\psi_I(t) angle = U_0^\dagger(t, t_0) |\psi(t) angle \]

This transformation simplifies dynamics when \(V(t)\) is weak or slowly varying.

\[ rac{d}{dt} |\psi_I(t) angle = - rac{i}{\hbar} V_I(t) |\psi_I(t) angle \]

\[ V_I(t) = U_0^\dagger(t, t_0) V(t) U_0(t, t_0) \]
